\begin{exercise}
Let $f$ be a function defined on $(0,2)$ such that $h(x)\le f(x)\le g(x)$ for all $x$ with $0<x<2$. Then $\lim_{x\to 1}f(x)$ \wordChoice{\choice[correct]{exists}\choice{does not exist}} due to \wordChoice{\choice{continuity}\choice{of the difference law}\choice{quotient law}\choice[correct]{the Squeeze Theorem}}.
\begin{exercise}
Now consider the quotient $\frac{h(x)}{g(x)}$. The form of the limit $\lim_{x\to 1^+}\frac{h(x)}{g(x)}$ is \wordChoice{\choice{determinate}\choice[correct]{indeterminate}}. In particular, it is of the form \wordChoice{\choice{nonzero over zero} \choice[correct]{zero over zero}}.
\begin{exercise}
Multiplying by the conjugate, it follows that for all $x>1$,  
\[
\frac{h(x)}{g(x)}=\frac{1}{\answer{\sqrt{x}+1}}.
\]
\begin{exercise}
Now, evaluate the limit:
\[
\lim_{x\to 1^+}\frac{h(x)}{g(x)}=\answer{\frac{1}{2}}.
\]
\end{exercise}
\end{exercise}
\end{exercise}
\end{exercise}
